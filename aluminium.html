steel

Aluminium is the most abundant metal in the Earth's crust. Since Aluminium metal is too reactive chemically to occur natively, it is found combined in over 270 different minerals, chief ore of aluminium being bauxite. Aluminium is remarkable for its low density and for its ability to resist corrosion. Structural components made from aluminium and its alloys are vital to the aerospace industry and are important in of transportation and structural materials.
